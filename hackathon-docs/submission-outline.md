## Title

Cross-Model Spec Comparison: Finding Disagreement in Candidate Lean 4 Specifications Generated by OpenAI Models

## Abstract Draft

We study whether candidate formal specifications produced by different OpenAI models agree on the intended behavior of small systems. For three specification tasks, we generate three Lean 4-oriented candidates per task, normalize them into a shared canonical format, and compare their assumptions, preconditions, postconditions, and invariants. We then search for concrete counterexamples using executable validators and property-based fuzzing. Our results show that even when the models appear aligned at a high level, they often diverge on boundary conditions and hidden assumptions. The project demonstrates a lightweight workflow for turning model disagreement into actionable evidence of underspecification, and it provides a reproducible prototype for specification validation under a small budget and a short time horizon.

## Introduction
